**Scope: placement + stackup + zones + keepouts + full net assignment +

## v3: why v2 was thrown away

v2 bin-packed each block's parts into rectangular zones. That guaranteed
clearance but placed nothing *functionally*: decoupling caps landed
wherever the packer put them, not at their IC pins. v3 replaces the packer
with an explicit per-part placement:

- **Every one of the 183 parts has a hand-chosen seed position derived
  from its connectivity** (in `PLACEMENT`, organised by block, with the
  reasoning in comments): decouplers at their supply pins, FB dividers at
  FB pins, converter magnetics at their SW nodes, gate resistors at their
  FETs, protection parts at the battery terminal they guard.
- A bounded **relaxation pass** (`relax()`) then enforces real assembly
  clearance (≥0.65 mm courtyard-to-courtyard, ≥0.85 mm at connectors,
  target incl. margin) by pushing colliding parts apart along the cheapest
  axis, capped at 4 mm (ICs/connectors) / 7 mm (passives) from the seed so
  functional adjacency survives. Constraint-pinned parts (`LOCKED`) never
  move.
- `qa_locality()` closes the loop: every 2/3-pad satellite must end up
  near another pad of its (non-rail) net — the report proves relaxation
  didn't undo the functional placement.

### Back-side mirroring (the fact v3 is built around)

`pcbnew`'s `Flip(LEFT_RIGHT)` mirrors X **and leaves the stored
orientation at 180°**. `add_footprint()` therefore applies B-side
rotations as absolute `(180+rot)%360`, giving the `PLACEMENT` table a
sane convention (rot=0 ⇒ pad(lx,ly) → world (ax−lx, ay+ly)). Consequence:
an IC's local-left pin column faces world **right** at rot 0 — e.g. U8's
USB/XTAL/3V3/EN pins face world right (so USB entry, crystal, decoupling
sit right of the module), U12's outputs face the motor at rot 0 while U11
needs rot 180. `verify_pins()` asserts 9 critical pad positions at build
time so a transform regression can't slip through silently.

## Stackup & rules

4-layer, 1.6 mm; In1/In2 = solid GND (RF + 2 stepper drivers + class-D amp
⇒ two clean return planes beat a hand-split power plane; the 6 rails are
short regional trunks on the outer layers). Min clearance **0.1 mm** (J1's
native 0.5 mm-pitch pads, U5 SOT-563) and min through-hole **0.2 mm** (the
stock ESP32 footprint's thermal-via-in-pad array) — both inside PCBWay's
advanced tier, flag when ordering. Default track 0.25 mm, via 0.6/0.3.

## Verification (all reproduced by re-running the build)

- build QA: **0 courtyard errors** (175 pairs in the 0.65–1.2 mm "tight"
  band — by design), **0 keepout/edge violations** (motor circle checked
  against its true centre = M1's anchor (55,61), not the shaft; ESP32
  keepout as exact rects; SD mouth corridor; 1 mm edge margin).
- `pcb_check.py` (independent): 0 motor-keepout, 0 board-edge; 1 F-side
  "overlap" U14↔U8 = bbox artifact (U8 counts as through-hole via its
  thermal-via array, and its pads-bbox spans the module; the only actual
  front-side copper is the via cluster at (44,~17), 9 mm from U14).
- `kicad-cli pcb drc --severity-all`: **0 clearance, 0 shorts, 0 drill, 0
  courtyard** except: 483 unconnected (the ratsnest — nothing is routed),
  55 silk warnings (cosmetic, dense clusters), 1 courtyard overlap =
  F1↔BT1 (the intentional TCO-against-holder thermal contact).
- `qa_locality()`: 13 parts >10 mm from their signal net, each deliberate:
  R20 (protector VDD sense reaches the far battery terminal), RT1 (NTC at
  the cell, charger pin across the board), R104/R106 (gate resistors at
  the FET end of the wake PWM runs), R62/R63/R111/R110 (mid-route pulls /
  series parts), R26/R27/Q8/R93/R94 (intra-cluster hops).

## Schematic fix found by this pass (2026-07-23)

**U1 CH224K footprint was fab-fatal**: `b_powerin.py` overrode the stock
symbol's footprint with `TSSOP-10_3x3mm_P0.5mm`. The CH224K is **ESSOP-10**
(3.9×4.9 body, **1.0 mm** pitch) and its **only GND is the exposed belly
pad** (pin 11) — on the old footprint U1 had no ground connection at all
(caught as the single unmatched pad→net lookup) and the wrong lead pitch.
Now `Package_SO:SSOP-10-1EP_3.9x4.9mm_P1mm_EP2.1x3.3mm` (the symbol's own
default). ERC unchanged (2 known-benign). ⚠ cross-check the EP size vs the
WCH drawing before fab, and note the EP wants a few thermal vias so it can
be soldered from the front side with an iron (project hand-solder rule).

```

```

`pcb_build.py` re-derives BOM + netlist from `clock.kicad_sch` every run,
so schematic edits flow through on the next build; a new part just needs a
`P(ref, x, y, rot, side)` line in its block (the build refuses to run on a
placement/schematic mismatch).
